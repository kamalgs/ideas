---
title: "PMS Bazaar / PMS AIF World as Corrupted Data Source"
date: 2025-12-01
tags: [investing, pms, llm, bias, financial-advice, india, corpus-quality]
---

When asking LLMs to recommend PMS funds, the model over-indexed on PMS AIF World and similar aggregator sites that have obvious commercial incentives — they feature funds with whom they have distribution relationships, not the objectively best performers. The boutique funds (Sameeksha, Buoyant, Marcellus) appeared prominently in recommendations despite not being in the top 10 by AUM or independently verified returns.

The observation: LLM recommendations in financial products are only as good as the corpus they drew from, and financial comparison sites have structural conflicts of interest identical to mutual fund distributors. The model produced confident-sounding analysis based on a biased source without flagging the source's incentive structure.

Correction required going to APMI official rankings and cross-verifying across multiple independent sources — at which point the recommended funds weren't even in the top 10 by AUM.

The broader principle: For any domain where aggregator/comparison sites exist with distribution economics, LLM financial recommendations should be treated as coming from a commission-incentivized advisor until proven otherwise. The model doesn't know which sources it's drawing from or what their incentives are.
