The observation: Tesla stock inflating from $200 to $400 on robotaxi fantasies is functionally identical to government money printing. Owners feel wealthier, borrow against inflated collateral, spend more, chase the same goods — demand increases without corresponding productive output. Money supply effectively expands. The mechanisms are identical.

The class interest that explains the asymmetric reaction:

**Government printing** → benefits debtors, workers, bottom 50%. Wages can rise, debt gets devalued, fixed-income assets (held by the wealthy) lose real value.

**Asset inflation** → benefits creditors, owners, top 10%. Wealth concentrates upward, collateral expands for leveraged buying, no wage pressure, no labor market tightening.

The "sound money" crowd owns assets. They celebrate the mechanism that enriches them and condemn the mechanism that enriches others, framing both positions as economic principle rather than class interest.

The historical evidence is clean: 1970s inflation with wage-price spiral — capitalists funded Volcker's brutal rate hikes. 2010-2021 QE with asset price explosion and stagnant wages — same people called for more easing. Same monetary dynamics, opposite reactions. The difference was who benefited.

The Austrian School tell: Developed by von Mises and Hayek — aristocrats in post-WWI Austria horrified by social democracy. The economics was reverse-engineered to justify their class position. Deflationary "sound money" helps creditors, hurts debtors. That's not a coincidence.

What's genuinely novel here: Not the political economy analysis (which has precedent in Piketty, Graeber etc.) but the specific formulation — Tesla at $400 on robotaxi speculation as functionally equivalent money printing, just with a different distribution mechanism. The equivalence is obvious once stated but rarely stated this cleanly.
