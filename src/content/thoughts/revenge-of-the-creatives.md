---
title: "Revenge of the Creatives"
date: 2026-03-01
tags: [ai, llm, copyright, open-source, dns, authentication, creative-economy]
---

Commercial LLMs trained on creative work without consent or compensation. Indie creators — filmmakers, writers, musicians — are now walling off new content. This defensive posture, combined with a Wikipedia-style consented open corpus and DNS-based agent authentication (DKIM/SPF for agents), becomes an alternative AI knowledge architecture. Open base model + curated consented retrieval layer beats commercial models trained on SEO slop.

The DKIM analogy: a DNS TXT record declares authorized agent identities. Request carries signed token. Corpus endpoint verifies. Pass or fail. No new CA hierarchy needed — DNS is already the distributed trust layer.

Core insight: The creatives' defensive move becomes the foundation of a better architecture, not just a protest.
