Rang De's growth bottleneck isn't regulatory — it's conversion. HNI lenders receive fractional repayments (₹83 hits their savings account 40 times a month mixed with everything else) and the mental friction kills reinvestment.

The insight: e₹ special purpose wallet as a dedicated, ringfenced corpus account. Repayments land there automatically, compound via reinvestment mandate, never touch the lender's bank account unless explicitly withdrawn. The corpus lives separately — can't accidentally spend it on Swiggy.

The specific composition that's novel: Rang De as NBFC-P2P qualifies for "Sponsor" status in ICICI's CBDC architecture — meaning Rang De could issue programmable e₹ to lender wallets restricted to flow back only to Rang De's VPA. Auto-reinvestment mandate built into the token itself, not a separate standing instruction.

Infrastructure already closer than expected: UPI ↔ e₹ interoperability live today. Special purpose wallets in ICICI's stack. MobiKwik/CRED established non-bank CBDC wallet precedent via Yes Bank as sponsor.

Two specific asks needed: Rang De institutional e₹ wallet + Sponsor status from their bank partner; RBI opening third-party mandate APIs (business-to-CBDC push) equivalent to UPI AutoPay for CBDC.

Why it's non-trivial: Requires simultaneously knowing Rang De's business model, NBFC-P2P regulatory position, e₹ programmability architecture, and the ICICI Sponsor framework — none of which individually suggests the others.
