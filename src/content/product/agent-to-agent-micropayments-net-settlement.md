---
title: "Agent-to-Agent Micropayments via Net Settlement"
date: 2026-03-01
tags: [ai, agents, payments, upi, micropayments, india, cbdc, fintech]
---

Micropayments for content retrieval have failed historically due to transaction costs. Agent-to-agent communication removes human friction from the payment loop. Net settlement (no actual cash per transaction, just accounting entries — exactly how Visa and exchange clearing works) collapses per-transaction cost.

UPI extension or a UPI-style intermediary for the agentic economy. NPCI has shown willingness to innovate faster than Western regulators. If any country standardizes agentic micropayments on fiat rails rather than crypto, India is the most likely candidate.

Later refinement: Payments may be unnecessary entirely. Wikipedia model — consent-based open corpus, community governed, no payment infrastructure — is more robust and more likely to actually happen. Stripping payments removes the coordination problem that kills good ideas.

The cryptographic coupling idea: Dynamically signed tokens where key retrieval from intermediary carries implicit payment promise. Content delivery and payment atomically coupled in the protocol. Elegant but may be overengineered relative to the Wikipedia model.
