Inverting Maslow — Capability Infrastructure as the Lever

thoughts 1 January 2026
maslowcapabilityindiadevelopmentgreen-revolutionm-pesait-generation

The standard Maslow reading: satisfy lower needs before pursuing higher ones. The inversion: providing capability infrastructure that addresses lower needs frees people to pursue higher ones without the provider needing to engineer outcomes.

The thought experiment: Today’s world has a top 5-10% living comfortably by extracting from the remaining 90%. What if exceptional capability created obligation rather than extraction rights? Capability as structural obligation rather than privilege. Practical mechanisms: differential taxation based on local inequality (direct feedback loop between conditions and obligation), non-transferable non-inheritable social credit.

The distinction from eugenics: self-actualization is a psychological achievement and developmental process, not an inherited trait. The capacity exists in all humans — the question is what conditions enable or suppress it.

The historical precedents that work: Green Revolution didn’t teach farmers to be rational — gave them better seeds that worked within their constraints. M-Pesa didn’t educate Kenyans about financial planning — provided a tool that worked within existing constraints. Oral rehydration therapy — simple, accessible, worked. Pattern: capability infrastructure working within constraints → adoption → condition improvement → capability expansion. No top-down engineering of outcomes.

The uncomfortable observation about India’s IT generation: The cohort that benefited from subsidized engineering education, public infrastructure, and reservation-adjacent opportunity now sends children to private schools, uses private hospitals, and is largely silent on rebuilding the enabling infrastructure at scale. The system that made them is not the system they’re building for others — not from malice but from rational individual behavior. The political constituency for rebuilding public capability infrastructure is weakest precisely among those who most viscerally understand its value.
